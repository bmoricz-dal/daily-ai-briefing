## 2026-05-10

### Main theme
AI and BI are converging around governed workflow execution: agents, semantic models, APIs, task flows, approvals, telemetry, and auditable action.

### Strongest insight
The business value question is shifting from “Can AI produce a good answer?” to “Can the organisation safely turn AI-supported insight into controlled action inside real workflows?”

### Strongest source signal
OpenAI’s Codex safety post and Microsoft Power BI’s DAX API / translytical task flow items point to the same strategic direction: enterprise AI value depends on sandboxing, permissions, semantic validation, workflow embedding, and auditability.

### Academic note
The strongest academic angle for business value measurement is the warning that adoption volume is not the same as transformation. The arXiv “When AI Meets Science” paper is useful as an early signal, but it remains a preprint and should not be treated as settled evidence.

### Source weaknesses
The source pack remains too dependent on company/vendor blogs and noisy academic metadata. OpenAlex returned weak, future-dated, duplicated, or irrelevant items. Missing categories remain official UK/regional sources, FMCG/distribution sources, reputable independent news, and professional analyst sources.

### Next issue angle
Move from adoption signals to value evidence: how SMEs and operational teams can measure whether AI improves decision quality, workflow speed, data quality, customer outcomes, or financial performance beyond anecdotal time savings.
